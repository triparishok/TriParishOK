Parish History

A faithful Catholic presence in Sterling

The first Mass for the Catholic community that became Our Lady of Perpetual Help was celebrated in a parishioner’s home by Rev. Urban de Hasque on August 21, 1902. Under the direction of Rev. William Huffer of Lawton, a church was established and built in 1904.

The early parish community was made up largely of farm families. A school was established in 1909, and the first rectory was built in 1910 during the residency of Rev. Anthony Van der Grinten.

After fire destroyed the church on December 22, 1928, the parish quickly began rebuilding. The present church was dedicated to Our Lady of Perpetual Help on October 8, 1929. The old school building still stands and now serves as a fellowship hall, religious education space, and parish activity space.

History adapted from the former Tri-Parish About page.
