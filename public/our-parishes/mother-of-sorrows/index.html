Parish History

A faithful Catholic presence in Apache

The Catholic history of Mother of Sorrows begins with Rev. Urban de Hasque, a young Belgian priest who first visited Apache on October 9, 1901. The first Catholic service in Apache was held on November 27, 1901, in George Rose’s building.

The congregation organized quickly, and a committee secured land for a church. During the next few years, the community built the church, working through financial challenges and questions about the best location.

After Christmas 1905, the church was moved to the location where it stands today. A rectory was built east of the church by Rev. Frederick Van der Aa, the first resident priest, who served there from 1907 to 1925.

History adapted from the former Tri-Parish About page.
