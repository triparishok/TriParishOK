Sacraments

The Sacraments

The Sacraments are sacred signs instituted by Christ and entrusted to the Church. Through them, God gives grace, strengthens His people, and draws us more deeply into His life.

Start Here

Need a Sacrament?

For Baptism, First Holy Communion, Confirmation, Marriage, Anointing of the Sick, or other sacramental needs, please contact the parish office.

The parish office can help you understand the next step, preparation process, and any needed paperwork.

Contact the Parish Office

Confession

Return to grace.

Confession is one of the great gifts of Christ to His Church. If it has been a long time, you are welcome to come back. The priest can help you through it.

Seven Sacraments

Grace for the whole Christian life

Each Sacrament is a real encounter with Christ through His Church.

New Life in Christ

Baptism

Baptism is the first Sacrament and the doorway into the Christian life. Parents seeking Baptism for a child should contact the parish office for preparation and scheduling.

Contact the Parish Office

Strengthened by the Holy Spirit

Confirmation

Confirmation completes baptismal grace and strengthens Catholics to live and witness the faith. Contact the parish office for preparation information.

Contact the Parish Office

Reconciliation

Confession

In Confession, Christ forgives sins through the ministry of the priest. Catholics are encouraged to confess regularly, especially before receiving Holy Communion if conscious of mortal sin.

View Confession Times

Source and Summit

Holy Eucharist

The Holy Eucharist is the Body, Blood, Soul, and Divinity of Jesus Christ. Preparation for First Holy Communion is arranged through the parish.

View Mass Times

Priesthood and Diaconate

Holy Orders

Through Holy Orders, bishops, priests, and deacons are ordained for service to Christ and His Church. Men discerning a vocation are encouraged to speak with the pastor.

Contact the Parish Office

Holy Matrimony

Marriage

Catholic marriage is a covenant ordered toward the good of the spouses and the welcoming of children. Couples should contact the parish before setting a wedding date or making final plans.

Contact the Parish Office

Healing and Strength

Anointing of the Sick

The Anointing of the Sick is for Catholics facing serious illness, surgery, weakness from age, or danger of death. Please contact the parish office for assistance.

Contact the Parish Office

Important

Parish preparation details may vary.

Sacramental preparation, paperwork, sponsor requirements, records, and scheduling should be confirmed directly with the parish office.

For urgent sacramental needs, especially danger of death, please contact the parish office as soon as possible.

Contact the Parish Office
